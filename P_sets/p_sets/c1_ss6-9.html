06. There are eight trophies $(t_1,t_2,t_3,\dots,t_8)$ being awarded. Generally, we will be considering linear permutaions of size eight selected from groups of size $n.$
(a) If thirty people enter the race, we are considering permutations of thirty objects, taken eight at a time. That is, $P(30,8).$

$ \require{cancel} \begin{split} \displaystyle \frac{n!}{(n-r)!} &= \frac{30!}{(30-8)!} \\ &= \frac{30\cdot29\cdot28\cdots23\cdot \color{grey}{\cancel{22!}}}{\color{grey}{\cancel{22!}}} \\ &= \frac{30!}{22!}. \\ &\ \square \end{split} $

(b) Roberta and Candice may finish among the top three runners in $3!=6$ ways. For each of these, there are $P(28,6)$ ways for the remaining $6/8$ top runners to finish the race. By the rule of product, there are $6 \cdot P(28,6)$ ways to award the trophies with these two runners among the top three finalists. $\square$
