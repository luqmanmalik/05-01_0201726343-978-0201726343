30. A sequence of letters of the form abcba, where the expression is unchanged upon revising order, is an example of a palindrome (of five letters).
  1. If a letter may appear more than twice, how many palindromes of five letters are there? of six letters?
  2. Repeat part (a) under the condition that no letter may appear twice.

35.
  1. In how many ways can seven people be arranged about a circular table?
  2. If two of the people insist on sitting next to each other, how many arrangements are possible?
36.
  1. In how many ways can eight people, denoted $A,B,\dots,H$ be seated about the square table shown in the figure below?
  2. If two of the eight people, say $A$ and $B$, do not get along well, how many different seatings are possible with $A$ and $B$ not sitting next to each other?
    Note: this result reveals that the combinatorics of seating/placing people around the square table is congruent with placing them about the circular table.
